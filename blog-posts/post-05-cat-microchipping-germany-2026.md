# Cat Microchipping in Germany: What Changed in 2025/2026

If you have a cat in Germany, this one's for you.

For years, cat registration in Germany was a patchwork of voluntary schemes and loose local rules — dramatically different from the dog registration system, which comes with a compulsory chip, tax (*Hundesteuer*), and a whole bureaucratic paper trail. Cats largely slipped through.

That's changing. Across Germany and at the EU level, rules around cat identification and registration are tightening, with several German states moving towards compulsory microchipping and registration for outdoor cats. Here's exactly where things stand in 2026 — and what you need to do if you haven't already chipped and registered your cat.

---

## Why Cat Microchipping Is Now a Serious Topic

Two things happened in quick succession:

**1. The stray cat problem became undeniable.** Germany has an estimated 2 million stray cats — many of them former pets abandoned when owners moved away. Shelters in major cities are chronically overfull. Without microchips, returning lost cats to their owners is nearly impossible, and reunification rates remain very low.

**2. EU-level pressure to standardise.** The EU Pet Travel Regulation (EU 576/2013) already requires microchipping for pets crossing borders. Several EU member states have moved to compulsory domestic cat microchipping (England introduced mandatory microchipping for cats in 2024, joining Scotland and several other countries). Germany's animal welfare bodies have been lobbying for similar rules domestically.

The result: **a growing number of German states and municipalities are now requiring or strongly recommending microchipping for outdoor cats**, with full mandatory schemes under active discussion at federal level.

---

## The Current State of the Law (2026)

Germany does not yet have a single federal law mandating cat microchipping. But the picture varies significantly by state:

**States with mandatory or near-mandatory cat microchipping rules:**
- **North Rhine-Westphalia (NRW)** — mandatory microchipping and TASSO registration required for outdoor cats under the state's animal welfare provisions
- **Lower Saxony** — mandatory chip and registration for outdoor/free-roaming cats
- **Saarland** — mandatory chip and registration
- **Several municipalities in Bavaria, Baden-Württemberg, and Brandenburg** have local ordinances requiring microchipping

**States with strong official recommendations (not yet legally enforced):**
- Berlin, Hamburg, Munich, Frankfurt: official recommendation by local authorities, with penalties possible for animal welfare violations if an unchipped cat causes problems (e.g. as part of a feral colony crackdown)

**Indoor-only cats:**
In most German states, indoor-only cats are not currently subject to mandatory microchipping requirements. However, if your cat ever gets out — even briefly — you'll be glad the chip was there. Lost cats without microchips are routinely handed to shelters and rehomed when unclaimed.

**The direction of travel:** Federal legislation is expected to align Germany with the NRW/Lower Saxony approach within the next 1–2 years. If you have a cat in Germany and haven't microchipped yet, do it now — it's €30–50, takes two minutes at any vet, and will be required regardless.

## What Microchipping Actually Involves

**The procedure:** A sterile chip the size of a grain of rice is inserted under the skin, typically at the back of the neck, via a needle. It takes seconds. No anaesthetic is needed. Most cats barely react.

**The chip standard:** All chips used in Germany must conform to **ISO 11784/11785** — a 15-digit code. This is the international standard, meaning your cat can be scanned anywhere in Europe with a standard reader.

**The cost:** €25–50 at a vet, sometimes slightly less at shelter-run chip days. The fee may include registration — confirm when you book.

**Does it hurt?** Briefly. Most vets describe it as comparable to a vaccination. Cats usually forget about it within minutes.

## Registration: The Two Databases You Need to Know

A chip is only useful if it's registered. In Germany, there are two main national databases:

### TASSO (Tierschutzorganisation)
- The largest free pet database in Germany
- Register at [tasso.net](https://www.tasso.net) — registration is free
- Widely used by shelters, vets, and animal welfare organisations
- When a lost animal is found and scanned, TASSO is typically the first database checked

### FINDEFIX
- The official database of the German Kennel Club (VDH)
- Also used by many vets and shelters
- Registration costs a small annual fee
- Good secondary registration alongside TASSO

**Recommendation: register with both.** It's free for TASSO and inexpensive for FINDEFIX. The 10 minutes it takes could mean the difference between your cat coming home and not.

### What to include in your registration:
- Your cat's microchip number (15 digits — written in your EU Pet Passport after chipping)
- Your name, address, and **two phone numbers** (including a mobile)
- Your cat's photo
- Any medical conditions or medication your cat takes

**Important for expats:** Update your registration immediately every time you move. A TASSO record pointing to an old address is nearly useless. Log in and update it — it takes two minutes.

## The EU Pet Passport for Cats

If you travel internationally with your cat — or arrived in Germany from another EU country — you need an EU Pet Passport (*EU-Heimtierausweis*). This document:

- Records the microchip number
- Contains vaccination records (rabies vaccination is required for cross-border travel)
- Is issued by any registered vet in Germany
- Travels with your cat for life

If your cat doesn't have a passport yet, your vet can issue one at the same appointment where the chip is implanted.

## Getting Your Cat Microchipped: Practical Steps

1. **Book a vet appointment** — any vet can implant a microchip. You don't need a specialist.
2. **Ask for ISO-compliant chip** — standard in Germany but worth confirming
3. **Get an EU Pet Passport issued at the same appointment** if your cat doesn't have one
4. **Register with TASSO** (free, at tasso.net) as soon as you get home — you'll need the 15-digit chip number from your passport or vet receipt
5. **Register with FINDEFIX** as a backup
6. **Update your details** any time you move

## FAQs

**My cat already has a chip from the UK/US/Australia — do I need a new one?**
Not necessarily. If the chip is ISO 11784/11785 compliant (15-digit), it's valid in Germany. Have your vet scan and confirm the standard, and make sure it's registered on a German database (TASSO at minimum). If the chip is a different standard (some older US chips use 10-digit codes), talk to your vet about options.

**My cat is indoor-only — do I still need to chip them?**
Not legally required in most states — yet. But practically, yes. Cats escape. Windows get left open. Indoor cats that get out are the hardest to recover because they have no experience outdoors and can travel far in a panic. A €40 chip is good insurance.

**My landlord says I need to prove my cat is registered — what do they need?**
Show them the EU Pet Passport with the chip number recorded, and a screenshot or printout of your TASSO registration. That's typically sufficient.

**I found a cat — how do I check if it's chipped?**
Take it to any vet, shelter, or Tierarztpraxis — they can scan for a chip for free. They'll check TASSO and FINDEFIX to find the owner.
