# Public Transport with Dogs in Cologne: The KVB Guide

Cologne (*Köln*) is a genuinely dog-friendly city. The Rhine promenade, the Stadtwald, the Grüngürtel parks - there's no shortage of places to take your dog. But getting there? That means the KVB, the *Kölner Verkehrs-Betriebe*, Cologne's public transport network.

Here's everything you need to know about taking your dog on Cologne's trams, U-Bahn, and buses. No stress, no fines, and no accidentally breaking rules you didn't know existed.

---

## The Short Version

- ✅ Dogs are allowed on all KVB services (trams, Stadtbahn/U-Bahn, buses)
- 🎫 Your dog needs a ticket, either a *Kurzstrecke* (short trip) or a child's ticket (*Kinderticket*)
- 🦮 Dogs must be on a leash at all times
- 😷 Muzzle required on large dogs in certain circumstances (read on)
- 🚫 Dogs cannot occupy seats

---

## Tickets: What to Buy for Your Dog

Your dog does not ride for free on the KVB (unlike in Hamburg, where dogs travel at no charge). You'll need to buy a separate ticket for them.

**The standard dog ticket is a *Kinderticket* (child's ticket) or a *Kurzstrecke* (short trip ticket).** In practice, most KVB riders buy a short trip ticket for their dog for journeys within the inner zones.

**Ticket options:**
| Ticket type | What it covers | Approximate cost |
|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Kurzstrecke | Up to 4 stops | ~€2.20 |
| Kinderticket (single) | All zones for one journey | ~€2.10 |
| Tageskarte (day ticket) | All-day travel | A single day ticket covers you + 1 dog for the day |

**Key rule:** One dog per adult ticket or day ticket is covered. If you have two dogs, you need two dog tickets.

**Tip for frequent KVB users:** The KVB app (*KVB-Fahrplanauskunft*) allows you to add a dog ticket when purchasing your own ticket. It's quicker than using the machines, especially when your hands are full.

---

## Leash and Muzzle Rules

**Leash:** Required at all times. Short leash, not a long retractable lead that your dog can use to explore the carriage.

**Muzzle:** The KVB's own rules don't mandate muzzles for all dogs, but **large dogs classified as "Großhunde"** (generally over 50cm at the shoulder) may be required to wear one depending on the situation. In practice, inspectors rarely enforce this for well-behaved dogs. If your dog is large, reactive, or anxious around other passengers, carrying a muzzle is wise and avoids any confrontation.

Dogs classified as **restricted breeds** (see our [breed restrictions guide](/blog/breed-restrictions-germany)) are required to wear a muzzle in all public spaces in most German states, including public transport regardless of individual transport authority rules.

---

## Peak Hours and Practical Tips

**Rush hour (7-9am and 4-7pm):** Avoid if you can. KVB carriages get crowded during peak commuting times, and a dog in a packed U-Bahn is uncomfortable for everyone, including your dog. The U-Bahn lines under the city centre (especially the Dom/Hauptbahnhof interchange) get particularly dense.

**The best KVB lines for dogs:**
- The **tram lines** (*Straßenbahn*) are generally roomier than the underground sections of the Stadtbahn and easier to board with a dog
- **Line 16/18** towards Bonn runs largely overground after leaving the city centre: spacious and dog-friendly
- **Buses** tend to have more floor space near the doors; position yourself there

**Avoid the front door of buses.** The area near the driver is the one place KVB drivers have been known to be fussy about dogs. Board from the middle or rear doors.

---

## What About Carriers and Small Dogs?

Small dogs that fit in a carrier or bag travel for free and don't need their own ticket; they're treated the same as luggage. The carrier must be fully enclosed (no dog sticking their head out in a way that would alarm other passengers).

**No carrier?** Small dogs on a leash are fine, but they still need a ticket like any other dog.

---

## The Ticket Inspector Question

KVB inspectors (*Kontrolleure*) do check dog tickets. It's not as frequent as on the S-Bahn, but it happens, particularly on the busy inner-city lines. The fine for travelling without a valid dog ticket is the same as for a person travelling without a ticket: currently **€60**.

Don't chance it. A Kurzstrecke ticket is €2.20.

## Dog-Friendly KVB Journeys Worth Making

With your KVB ticket in hand, here are the best dog-friendly trips in Cologne:

🌳 **Stadtwald** - Take the tram towards Müngersdorf. Cologne's largest urban forest, with off-leash meadows and forest trails. Perfect for a weekend morning.

🌊 **Rheinpark** - U-Bahn to Deutz/Messe or Köln-Messe/Deutz, then a short walk to the river. Dogs are welcome on the grass areas along the Rhine promenade.

🏡 **Grüngürtel** - Cologne's famous green belt circles the city. Multiple tram stops give access to different sections, all well-suited for dogs.

🐾 **Fühlinger See** - Further out (bus or tram plus a short walk), this lake complex has areas where dogs can swim, though leash rules apply in some designated sections.

## If Your Dog Has a Vet Appointment

Getting to a vet appointment by public transport in Cologne is completely doable; most of the practices in our [Cologne vet directory](/vets/cologne) are within easy reach of KVB lines.

A few practical notes:
- **Bring a carrier if your cat or small dog is stressed**: enclosed carriers significantly reduce anxiety on public transport
- **For post-surgery or unwell animals**, a taxi is kinder than the U-Bahn, even if the dog technically can travel
- **Larger dogs after surgery**: support them on the floor of the vehicle; don't lift them onto seats
