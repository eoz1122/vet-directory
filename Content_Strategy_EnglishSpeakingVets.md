# Content Strategy: EnglishSpeakingVets
**englishspeakinggermany.online** · April 2026

---

## Strategic North Star

Every piece of content should do at least one of these three things:

1. **Drive directory traffic** — help expat pet owners find that a verified vet exists and visit the directory
2. **Build topical authority** — signal to Google that this site is the definitive English-language resource for pet ownership in Germany
3. **Earn links and shares** — create content expat communities will share in Facebook groups, WhatsApp chats, and Reddit threads

The blog isn't a side project. It's the engine that gets the directory pages ranked.

## Content Brief Template

Use this structure for every new post. It keeps SEO baked in from the start.

```
TITLE: [Target keyword + emotional hook, under 60 chars for SEO]
URL: /blog/[keyword-slug-no-year]
TARGET KEYWORD: [Primary keyword]
SECONDARY KEYWORDS: [2-3 variations]
SEARCH INTENT: [Informational / Navigational / Commercial]
WORD COUNT TARGET: [600–800 for quick guides | 1,200–2,000 for pillar posts]

H1: [Same as or close to title]
META DESCRIPTION: [150–160 chars — what the reader will learn + one CTA]

MANDATORY INTERNAL LINKS:
- Link to /vets/[most relevant city] within first 300 words
- Link to 1–2 related blog posts
- Link to homepage or blog hub in final section

STRUCTURE:
- Intro (50–100 words): Hook with the pain point, preview the answer
- [H2 sections as needed]
- [Practical action steps in a scannable format]
- Directory CTA section: "Now find a vet near you →" with links to city pages
- Related guides footer

SCHEMA TO ADD: Article (all posts) | FAQ (for how-to and bureaucracy posts)
```

## The Emergency Vets Series — Your Biggest Opportunity

This is the single highest-ROI content type you could produce right now.

**Why:** When someone googles "emergency vet berlin english" at 11pm on a Sunday, they're in a state of distress, they will click the first result that looks trustworthy, and they will remember the site forever if it helped them. This drives word-of-mouth and direct traffic that no amount of SEO work can replicate.

**Format for each city post:**

```
Title: Emergency Vets in [City] — English-Speaking 24-Hour Clinics
URL: /blog/emergency-vet-[city]-english

Structure:
- What counts as a pet emergency (quick list)
- 24h English-speaking clinics in [City] — name, address, phone, hours
  (pull from your verified directory where possible)
- What to say when you call (German phrases + English explanation)
- Costs: what to expect for emergency visits in Germany
- After-hours tips: what if there's no 24h vet near you?
- CTA: "Browse all verified vets in [City] →"
```

**Cities to cover:** Berlin, Hamburg, Munich, Frankfurt, Cologne, Stuttgart, Düsseldorf, Leipzig

## Publishing Cadence & Workflow

**Target:** 4 posts per month (1 per week)

**Recommended workflow per post:**
1. Write to brief (targeting 1,200–1,800 words for pillar posts; 600–800 for quick guides)
2. Add internal links to at least 2 directory pages and 1 related blog post
3. Add article-specific title tag + meta description (not the generic one)
4. Add Article schema (JSON-LD)
5. Add FAQ schema if the post has a Q&A section (most do)
6. Compress images to WebP, add descriptive alt text
7. Publish → immediately request indexing via Google Search Console URL Inspection

**Don't:** Publish and forget. Check Search Console 2 weeks after each post to confirm it's indexed and note any early ranking signals.
